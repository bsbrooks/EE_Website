THURGOOD MARSHALL TIMELINE

 

July 2, 1908: Born in Baltimore, Maryland.

1930: Graduates with honors from Lincoln University. Aspires to attend University of Maryland Law School but told not to bother applying because of school's segregation policy.

1933: Graduates first in class from Howard University Law School.

1934: Begins work for Baltimore branch of the NAACP.

1936:With friend and mentor Charles Hamilton Houston, wins first major civil rights case, Murray v. Pearson. This ruling desegregated the University of Maryland Law School, the school Marshall was not allowed to attend.

1936: Becomes Assistant Special Counsel for NAACP in New York.

1940-1961: Serves as Legal Director of NAACP. In 1940, wins first Supreme Court victory.

1951: Visits South Korea and Japan at request of President Truman.Investigates charges of racism in the U.S. Armed Forces. Recognizes practice of "rigid segregation."

1954: Wins Brown v. Board of Education, the landmark lawsuit that ends legal segregation

January, 1960: Goes to Kenya at request of Tom Mboya, leader of the black Kenyans involved in independence movement to help draft constitution for Kenya's independence.

1961: Becomes second African American appointed to U.S.Court of Appeals, 2nd Circuit. All of his 112 rulings upheld by Supreme Court.

1965: Become first African American appointed U.S.Solicitor General by President Lyndon Johnson; wins 14 of the 19 cases he argued for the government between 1965-1967.

1967: Becomes first African American appointed to U.S. Supreme Court,

1991: Retires from Supreme Court citing health issues.

January 24, 1993: Dies at 84.
