Secondary Sources

 

           
            American RadioWorks.  Thurgood Marshall Before the Court.  Available
<http://americanradioworks.publicradio.org/features/marshall/>.  Retrieved December 27, 2008.

This website provided a comprehensive history of Thurgood Marshall’s life.

American Treasures of the Library of Congress.   A Pillar of Justice.  <http://select.nytimes.com/mem/archive/pdf?res=F20712FE39541A7A93C3A8178AD85F448685F9>.  February 4, 2009.

            This website provided a cartoon The Pillar of Justice which was used on the website.

Bland, Randall W.  Private Pressure on Public Law. The Legal Career of Justice Thurgood Marshall. Port Washington:  Kennikat Press, 1973.

              I used this biography to learn how Thurgood Marshall worked within the system
of  government to achieve social reform.  This book included briefs, government documents, and interviews.

Brown Foundation for Educational Equity, Excellence and Research. 
            < http://brownvboard.org/summary/>. February 3, 2009.
             
              This website provided information on Brown v. Board of Education.

            George Mason University.  Thurgood Marshall Supreme Court Justice. <http://chnm.gmu.edu/courses/122/hill/marshall.htm>. September 5, 2008.

                          This website provided a timeline of important events in the career of Thurgood Marshall.

Greenberg, Jack.  Crusaders in the Courts: How a Dedicated Band of Lawyers Fought for the Civil Rights Revolution. New York: Basic Books, 1994.

              This book gave information on the years of legal strategy leading to Brown v. Board of Education.  It also led me to interview Mr. Greenberg.

 

Justice for All:  Thurgood Marshall.  http://www.america.gov/media/pdf/books/marshall.pdf.  December 26, 2008.

This website provided several articles on the legacy of Thurgood Marshall including the Bill of Rights in Kenya that Marshall helped play a role in.  It also had a valuable timeline which was useful in helping me to create my own timeline. 

 

Landmarkcases.org. Brown v. Board of Education. <http://www.landmarkcases.org/brown/marshall.html>.  December 26, 2008.

            This website provided excellent information on Brown v. Board of Education.

Seattle Post-Intelligencer.  Thurgood Marshall’s Chair. < http://www.greenberg-
             art.com/.Toons/.Toons,%20favorites/Thurgood.html>.   February 4, 2009.

This website had a cartoon which was used on the website.

Thurgood Marshall. <http://www.thurgoodmarshall.com/home.htm>.  December 27, 2008.

            This is the website which relates to the book Thurgood Marshall by Juan Williams. There were interviews and speeches of Thurgood Marshall which were invaluable to my website.

Thurgood Marshall: Portrait of an American Hero. PBS Home Video, 1985.

            This video provided video footage which I used in the website.

Thurgood Marshall Scholarship Fund.  Brown v. Board of Education- Its impact on       Public Education. Brooklyn: Word for Word Publishing Co., Inc. 2005.

This was an invaluable pieces written by prominent individuals on the impact of Brown v. Board of Education.

Tushnet, Mark V.   Making Constitutional Law.  Thurgood Marshall and the Supreme Court, 1961-1991.  New York:  Oxford University Press, 1997.

              I used this book to examine the second half of Thurgood Marshall’s career.  It is a source written by one of Thurgood Marshall’s law clerks.  It is an important book as it prompted me to interview the author.

Williams, Juan.  Thurgood Marshall.  American Revolutionary. New York:  Time Books, 1998.

            I used this biography which had many photographs and conversations between the author and Marshall.  The book also prompted me to interview the author.
